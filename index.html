Cashew's origin is in Brazil, but today, countries like India, Vietnam, Nigeria, Ivory Coast, and Tanzania are among the largest producers. In India, its production is mainly found in states like Goa, Kerala, Karnataka, Maharashtra, and Odisha. The cashew tree grows up to 10-14 meters tall, with thick, evergreen leaves. The tree bears a cashew apple, which is red or yellow in color, and beneath it hangs a seed (nut), which is the actual cashew. A mature cashew tree can produce 40-50 kg of raw cashews annually, but only 250-300 grams of edible cashews are obtained from one kilogram of raw cashews, as the hard shell is removed during processing. The cashew processing involves several steps, including harvesting, shell removal, roasting, and grading, ensuring high-quality cashews reach the market.
